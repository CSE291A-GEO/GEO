How should women’s running shoes fit?

The best women’s running shoes should feel like a natural extension of your foot that’s comfortable, snug, but never too tight. Ideally, your running shoe should fit with a ½ to a full thumb’s width of room between the front of the shoe and your big toe, leaving enough room to wiggle your toes freely without the shoes feeling loose.
The midfoot should feel snug, offering arch support without applying pressure to the top of your foot. Ensure your foot's arch aligns with the shoe's midsole arch. The heel cup should be snug to prevent slippage, which can lead to blisters and irritation, but it should not constrict your Achilles tendon.
 New Balance also offers a variety of widths including wide and extra wide women’s running shoes to ensure a comfortable fit. 

What is the best way to wash running shoes?

Spot cleaning is the best option for cleaning your running shoes and encouraging longevity. Opt for dish soap, hot water, and a rag (or an old toothbrush if you’re dealing with stubborn debris) to spot clean mud and stains from your running shoes. Then, stuff your shoes with newspaper and let them air dry—position them in front of a fan for faster results.Avoid using radiators, clothes dryers, or direct sunlight, as high heat can damage the shoes. While machine washing is generally not recommended as it can shorten the lifespan of your shoes, if you choose to machine wash, use a gentle cycle with cold water, mild detergent, and place shoes in a mesh bag or pillowcase. Always air dry thoroughly.For more thorough hand washing, first brush off any excess dirt. Then, scrub the outsole, midsole, and upper with a mild detergent and a soft-bristled brush or old toothbrush. For specific stains, a mixture of one part vinegar and two parts water can tackle grass stains, while a paste of one part baking soda and one part vinegar works well for trail dirt or coffee. For white shoes or midsoles, a Mr. Clean Magic Eraser can be effective. After cleaning, rinse the shoes with clean water and air dry in a well-ventilated area, potentially with brief sun exposure to help kill bacteria.

Understanding your pronation and arch type is crucial for selecting the right shoe to prevent injury and enhance comfort.

What are the best running shoes?

Like most shoes, finding the best running shoe for your preferences will depend on unique factors like your foot, arches, goals, and stability needs. The best running shoe will offer the ideal balance of softness and stability, comfortable fit, and the preferred amount of cushioning for comfort and longevity.

How do I break in my running shoes?

The perfect-fit running shoe still might require a bit of a break-in period for optimal wear. While this break-in period will likely be short, it’s best to allow a little time before lacing them up for a long run or a big race.
